    Started by Jeremy Fish

    To:

    Rep. Arnold, Rep. Blumenauer, Rep. Walden, Sen. Wyden, Sen. Merkley, Rep. Bonamici, (This list is open-ended. It is up to the users to determine who a response should be requested from when they vote on the letter.)

    Users have requested a response from each of these politicians. Each time users “up-vote” an OpenLetter, they select the politician(s) in their voting district from whom they want a response. When enough users have requested a response from a certain politician, PolitiCrowd automatically posts the OpenLetter to their PolitiCrowd profile, reminding them to act. This is the main text of the OpenLetter. Its purpose is to propose a particular course of action to legislators and to invite discussion. The letter could be designed to raise awareness for an issue, suggest new legislation, or request some other course of action. We encourage advocacy groups to post petition letters concerning their policy objectives in the form of OpenLetters, rather than ask their supporters to email thousands of copies of the same letter to politicians. Because we prompt politicians to respond to letters that receive enough support, it is imperative that users clearly state their opinions so that other users can both understand and support the letter. In order to be taken seriously by politicians and other members of the PolitiCrowd community, a letter should be short, polite, and to-the-point. Also, though it is important for users to air their grievances with an elected official’s actions, it is equally important for users to voice their support of their politicians’ initiatives. PolitiCrowd wants to build collaboration rather than discord.

    Sincerely,
    62 members of PolitiCrowd.
